About The Event

What CodeCamp: Bridgerland is

CodeCamp: Bridgerland is a community-driven, hands-on developer event for students, hobbyists, and professionals. Teams build projects, learn new tools, and connect with mentors from the local tech community.

It is designed to be beginner-friendly but still valuable for experienced builders, with practical challenges, peer collaboration, and guided support throughout the event.

1. Join a Team

Sign up solo or with friends, then form a team and pick a project idea.

2. Build and Learn

Work through the challenge with mentoring, workshops, and community help.

3. Demo Your Work

Share your final project, celebrate progress, and learn from other teams.

Event Info

What to expect at CodeCamp

From kickoff to final demos, the event is built to help teams ship something real while learning new tools and practices together.

Project-Based Learning

Every participant learns by building, with practical tasks that grow from beginner to advanced.

Mentor Support

Experienced developers and volunteers are available throughout the event to unblock teams quickly.

Friendly Competition

Teams can showcase their work, get feedback from judges, and celebrate creative problem-solving.

Community Connections

Meet local builders, students, and professionals, and leave with new contacts and future collaborators.

Team Structure

Teams are typically 2 to 4 participants, with at least one member available to present the final demo.

Event Rules

Projects should be built during the event window, follow the code of conduct, and clearly cite any third-party assets or AI tools used.

Competition Format

Rules, divisions, and teams

Based on our previous CodeCamp format, teams compete in a division based on the most experienced team member while following clear project and presentation rules.

Team Setup

You can register solo or with friends. Teams may include up to 4 participants.

  • Team size: 1 to 4 members
  • Cross-skill teams are encouraged
  • At least one member should present the final demo

Divisions

Teams are placed into one division according to the most experienced member.

  • K-12 Students
  • Collegiate Participants
  • Independent Developers
  • Industry Professionals

Core Rules

Projects should be fair, transparent, and completed primarily during the event window.

  • Follow the event code of conduct
  • Cite external assets, APIs, and AI-assisted work
  • Keep projects respectful, legal, and original

Quick Answers

Frequently asked questions

More details will be posted as the date approaches, but here are the basics most people ask first.

Who can join?

Everyone is welcome, from first-time coders to experienced developers.

Do I need a team before registering?

No. You can register solo and we will help you connect with a team.

What should I bring?

Bring a laptop, charger, and your curiosity. Project prompts and support will be provided.

How are teams organized?

Most teams have up to 4 people. You can join with friends or register solo and we will help place you.

What are the main project rules?

Keep projects respectful and legal, build primarily during the event, and disclose external code, APIs, and generated assets in your demo notes.
