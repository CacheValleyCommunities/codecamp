July 11th - 12th, 2025 — An Innovative Development Experience

Event Overview

We are pleased to announce the inaugural CodeCamp event on July 11-12, 2025 at Bridgerland Technical College. This two-day hackathon welcomes participants of all experience levels—from beginners to seasoned professionals. Develop games, applications, AI solutions, or hardware projects in an environment of innovation and collaboration.

This is a focused, efficient event. Just two 12-hour days of coding, networking, and building innovative solutions!

WHY PARTICIPATE

Have you been looking for an opportunity to develop your technical skills and showcase your abilities? This hackathon provides the perfect platform to build innovative projects while gaining experience and forming valuable industry connections in Northern Utah's tech sector.

INCLUSIVE ENVIRONMENT

Open to all skill levels, from students to senior professionals. Your unique perspective and talents are welcome.

FLEXIBLE PARTICIPATION

No mandatory team assignments. Form a group with colleagues or work independently based on your preference.

PROJECT AUTONOMY

Develop any project that enhances your skills without restrictive parameters limiting your creativity.

A COLLABORATIVE APPROACH

This event focuses on strengthening the community—not creating competition. Share knowledge, exchange ideas, and collaborate with others to achieve optimal results. Both newcomers and veterans will find mentors and colleagues to support them throughout the event.

PARTICIPATION DETAILS

Arrive Friday with project concepts. Depart Saturday with a functioning prototype of your creation. Whether it's a software application, a modern web solution, an AI implementation, or a hardware innovation, you'll have two full days to develop, refine, and present your work.

Throughout the event, acquire valuable knowledge, professional connections, and memorable experiences. (Professional development certificates will be provided upon completion.)

EVENT SCHEDULE

  • Day 1 (July 11): Project briefing, team formation, development kickoff, and specialized workshops
  • Day 2 (July 12): Project completion, presentations, networking event, and awards ceremony

PARTICIPANT CATEGORIES

Teams of 1-4 participants will compete within these four divisions. Your team's category is determined by your most experienced team member. All categories are free to enter.

K-12 STUDENTS

For K-12 students with developing skills. These young innovators bring fresh perspectives and unbounded creativity to their projects.

INDUSTRY PROFESSIONALS

For those working in the technology sector in any capacity, including internships. Experienced professionals seeking innovative challenges.

INDEPENDENT DEVELOPERS

For high school graduates and/or coding enthusiasts seeking collaborative opportunities who aren't in a formal educational program.

COLLEGIATE PARTICIPANTS

For those enrolled in programming-related courses at a collegiate level or higher who are developing their technical abilities.

Select your category carefully. Remember that you can form a team with participants from any background — your team's category will be determined by your most experienced member.

EVENT LOGISTICS

July 11-12, 2025 at Bridgerland Technical College. No predetermined projects—choose any development path. Individual participation or team formation; event mentors will be available to assist throughout your project. Categories for all experience levels ensure every participant has an opportunity to succeed.

NETWORKING & CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

As the event concludes on Day 2, our Networking & Career Fair will commence—an opportunity where participants and industry recruiters can connect and discover new professional opportunities.

INDUSTRY RECRUITERS

Local technology companies seeking talented professionals to join their teams. Present your completed projects and demonstrate your technical capabilities.

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT

Exchange insights with fellow participants and gather valuable knowledge from industry leaders during scheduled networking sessions.

CAREER OPPORTUNITIES

Discover potential internships, contract positions, and employment opportunities to continue your professional growth beyond the event.

NETWORKING FAIR SCHEDULE

  • Project Showcase (2:00-3:30 PM): Present your solutions to industry recruiters in a structured presentation format
  • Open Networking (3:30-5:00 PM): Connect with potential employers, mentors, and colleagues in an informal gathering
  • Awards Ceremony (8:00-9:00 PM): Recognition of outstanding projects and final networking opportunity

For those seeking employment opportunities after the hackathon, bring copies of your resume and be prepared to discuss your qualifications and completed projects. Company representatives will be available with contact information and future opportunities.
